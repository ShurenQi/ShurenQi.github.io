I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Department of Mathematics and the Center for Mathematical Artificial Intelligence (CMAI), The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), advised by Prof. Tieyong Zeng and Prof. Feng-Lei Fan. I obtained my Ph.D. degree from the Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (NUAA), supervised by Prof. Yushu Zhang, and also luckly advised by Prof. Jiantao Zhou and Prof. Xiaochun Cao.

My research interests cover computer vision, deep learning, and artificial intelligence, with a focus on robust and explainable representations in Geometric Deep Learning. I am also interested in their natural applications in forensic and security as well as AI for science. My current work attempts to give some new designs of invariant representations — from global to local and hierarchical assumptions. My research work has appeared in several top-tier venues such as ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR) and IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TPAMI).
